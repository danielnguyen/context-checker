# Disclaimer

SlopGuard is an experimental, local-first browser extension for surfacing presentation and transparency patterns in YouTube video cards.

It is provided for personal, educational, and experimental use. It is not a fact-checking service, trust-and-safety service, moderation system, legal service, or news-rating authority.

## No factual determinations

SlopGuard labels are heuristic or model-generated pattern indicators. They are not factual determinations and should not be treated as claims that any video, creator, channel, publisher, sponsor, advertiser, platform, or other party has engaged in deception, misinformation, disinformation, propaganda, fraud, illegality, bad faith, foreign influence, manipulation, or other misconduct.

A label means only that the local extension detected one or more observable signals, such as:

- sensational or high-engagement framing
- unclear or limited sourcing in visible metadata
- speculative claim patterns
- low-transparency presentation cues
- possible synthetic-style presentation cues
- visible sponsored-placement indicators

These signals may be wrong, incomplete, stale, context-dependent, or affected by YouTube layout changes.

## User responsibility

Users are responsible for interpreting SlopGuard outputs. Labels should be treated as prompts for further review, not as conclusions. Users should verify important claims through reliable primary or reputable secondary sources before relying on them.

Do not use SlopGuard output as proof of deception, misinformation, illegality, bad faith, or wrongdoing.

## Experimental software

SlopGuard may misclassify content, miss relevant content, duplicate labels, fail to detect sponsored placements, or display stale cached results. It may also break when YouTube changes its page structure.

The project is provided as-is, without warranties of accuracy, reliability, availability, fitness for a particular purpose, or non-infringement.

## Local-first design

SlopGuard is intended to operate as a local browser-extension layer. If model-assisted classification is enabled, users must supply their own API key and are responsible for any associated API usage, costs, data handling, and provider terms.
